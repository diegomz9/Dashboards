# Market Analysis Insights — 10 U.S. Companies (2025)

This document summarizes the key analytical insights  from a two-page Power BI dashboard analyzing **returns, trading volume, price behavior, etc** for 10 major U.S. companies during 2025.

---

## Page 1 — Market Overview (Returns & Trading Activity)

### 1. Overall Market Performance (Returns)

**Insight**  
Yearly Performance across the 10 companies was highly dispersed, with annual returns ranging from around **-0.6% to +65.9%**.

**Yearly Key takeaways**
- Only **3 out of 10 companies** exceeded a 20% annual return    
- Market performance favored **stock selection over sector-wide exposure**

---

### 2. Yearly Top Performers vs. Slow
(FYI, Return was calculated using First Day vs Last Day. Point-to-Point Return)

#### Yearly Top Performers
- **Google (+65.9%)**  
  Clear outlier, delivering nearly **2× the return** of the second-best performer
- **NVIDIA (+34.9%)**  
  Strong growth momentum aligned with heavy market participation
- **Walmart (+25.0%)**  
  A notable **defensive outperformer**, surpassing several large tech companies

**Insight**  
High returns were not exclusive to high-growth technology stocks. Walmart’s performance highlights **investor confidence in defensive business models** during periods of market uncertainty.

---

#### Yearly Underperformers
- **Amazon (+4.8%)**
- **PepsiCo (-0.6%)**

---

### 3. Yearly Trading Volume Analysis

**Highest trading volume**
1. **NVIDIA — 55.2B**
2. **Tesla — 24.4B**
3. **Apple — 13.5B**

**Lowest trading volume**
- **PepsiCo — 2.0B**
- **Meta — 3.9B**

**Insight**  
Trading activity was **highly concentrated in high-beta, narrative-driven stocks**, with NVIDIA alone accounting for a disproportionately large share of total market volume.

---

### 4. Yearly Return vs. Volume Relationship

#### High Return + High Volume
- **NVIDIA** — Strong returns supported by massive investor participation
- **Google** — Exceptional returns with consistent, moderate trading activity

**Interpretation**  
These stocks benefited from both fundamental performance and strong investor conviction.

---

#### High Volume + Moderate Returns
- **Tesla** — Very high volume but only moderate returns
- **Apple** — Strong trading activity with limited upside

**Interpretation**  
High trading volume does not guarantee superior returns. It may also reflect volatility, speculation, or profit-taking.

---

#### Low Volume + Stable Returns
- **Coca-Cola**
- **Walmart**

**Interpretation**  
Lower trading volumes combined with solid returns suggest long-term investor confidence rather than speculative trading.

---

### 5. Sector-Level Observations

**Technology sector**
- Wide dispersion in performance ( Google +65.9% vs. Amazon +4.8%)

**Consumer staples**
- Lower volatility and trading volume
- More stable but capped returns

---

## Page 2 — Single Stock & Price Behavior Analysis

### 1. Price Level Leadership (Yearly Average Close Price)

- **Meta** led with the highest yearly average close price (**$667.64**), peaking at **$760.83 in August**
- **Microsoft ($461.84)** and **Tesla ($356.54)** followed as avg close price high-priced leaders
- **Coca-Cola ($68.02)** and **Walmart ($98.58)** were the most affordable stocks

**Insight**  
Meta stands out as a premium-priced growth stock, while consumer staples remain accessible and stable.

---

### 2. Strongest Growth (January → December % Change)
(FYI, Growth was calculated based on AVG Monthly value. So, AVG January vs AVG December change)
- **Google (+60.6%)** — Clear growth leader
- **NVIDIA (+34.4%)** — Strong AI-driven momentum
- **Walmart (+22.8%)** and **Coca-Cola (+16.5%)** — Top non-tech performers
- Mid-tier gains: Apple, Tesla, Microsoft
- **Laggards**: Amazon (+0.5%), PepsiCo (+3.1%)

**Insight**  
Growth favored AI and innovation-driven companies, while defensive consumer stocks outperformed some major tech names in consistency.

---

### 3. Volatility & Risk Profile (Monthly High–Low Spread)

- **Highest volatility**: Meta, Tesla
- **Moderate volatility**: Microsoft
- **Lowest volatility**: Coca-Cola, Walmart, PepsiCo

**Insight**  
The data supports a clear segmentation between:
- **High volatility / high growth potential** (tech)
- **Low volatility / stability-oriented** stocks (consumer staples)

---

### 4. Seasonal & Monthly Trends

- **Spring dip (March–April)** observed across most stocks. Maybe political events. 
- **Strong recovery post-May**, especially among tech leaders
- **December prices exceeded January levels for 9 out of 10 companies**

**Insight**  
Many stocks followed a **V-shaped pattern**: early-year decline followed by strong recovery, highlighting the importance of timing and holding periods.

---

### 5. Tech vs. Consumer Staples Comparison

- **Tech stocks**: Higher prices, higher volatility, wider performance dispersion
- **Consumer staples**: Lower prices, low volatility, steady moderate growth

**Final Insight**  
Consumer staples provided **portfolio stability** during a volatile year, while tech stocks offered **higher upside at greater risk**, reinforcing a diversification narrative.

---

## Final Note

This analysis demonstrates how **combining returns, volume, price trends, and volatility** in an interactive Power BI dashboard enables deeper market understanding.
